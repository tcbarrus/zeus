Study Guide

Afer studying each chapter and learning from class lecture/discussion, you sould be able to...

Chapter 1: Food Choices and Human Health

  1. Identify the ways that nutrition affects health.
  2. Identify the six major nutrients, their roles in the body, and the calorie values of the three macronutrients and alcohol.
  3. List and define the 5 characteristics of a healthy diet.
  4. Use knowledge of the scientific method to find and recognize credible sources of information.
  5. Identify the stages of behavior change.
  6. Use conversion equivalents.

Chapter 2: Nutrition Tools: Standards and Guidelines (exclude Controversy 2)

  1. Define or describe DRI, RDA, AI, UL, EAR,, and AMDR and their uses.
  2. Use the DRI table to evaluate a person's nutrient intake levels.
  3. Identify the purposes of the Dietary Guidelines for Americans (DGA) and the benefits of following these recommendations.
  4. Use the USDA Food Pattern to classify foods and compare their nutritional value.
  5. Use the USDA Food Pattern to inform individualized dietary recommendations. This includes knowing
    1. Recommended amounts of foods from each group for the 2400 calorie level.
    2. USDA Food Pattern equivalents for fruits, vegetables, grains, milk, and protein foods (meat and meat alternates).
  6. Interpret information on food labels, including percent DV (daily value) and the descriptive terms high in, good source, healthy, and tree.

Chapter 3: The Remarkable Body

  1. Describe the major functions and location of the organs and secretions of the digestive system.
  2. Describe the processes of digestion (mechanical and chemical), absorption, transportation, and storage of nutrients.
  3. Identify the potential health benefits and negative consequences of alcohol consumption.

Chapter 4: The Carbohydrates: Sugar, Starch, Glycogen, and Fiber

  1. Know and use carbohydrate vocabulary, and identify food sources of digestible carbohydrates.
  2. Identify the benefits of fiber, its mechanisms, recommendations for intake, and food sources.
  3. Distinguish between refined, enriched, and whole-grain foods and identify examples of whole grains.
  4. Describe carbohydrate digestion and absorption and the body's use of glucose.
  5. Explain lactose intolerance and describe appropriate treatment.
  6. Discuss the types, causes, symptoms, and management of diabetes.
  7. Identify the current DRI and Dietary Guidelines for Americans recommendations for carbohydrate intake.
  8. Identify factors shown by research to cause obesity and diabetes, and identify factors that don't cause obesity.

Chapter 5: The Lipids: Fats, Oils, Phospholipids, and Sterols

  1. Describe functions of fat in the body and in foods.
  2. Describe the different types, structures, and functions of lipids including fatty acids.
  3. Describe lipid digestion, absorption, and transport in the body.
  4. Describe the health benefits or problems associated with consuming various lipids and identify food sources of different types of lipids including saturated -, monounsaturated-, and polyunsaturated- fatty acids, and cholesterol.
  5. Explain why plant oils are hydrogenated, identify foods likely to contain trans-fats, and describe the health implications of trans-fats.
  6. Identify the recommendations for fat intake from the Dietary Guidelines for Americans, the USDA Food Pattern, and the AMDR (Acceptable Macronutrient Distribution Range).

Chapter 6: The Proteins and Amino Acids

  1. Describe the structure of proteins and explain why adequate amounts of all the essential amino acids are required for protein synthesis.
  2. Describe protein digestion, absorption, and metabolism.
  3. List and describe the roles of protein in the body and explain nitrogen balance.
  4. Characterize foods in terms of protein amount and quality and also identify proteins that are complementary.
  5. State the AMDR for protein. Calculate an individual's recommended protein intake (DRI).
  6. Describe the consequences of consuming too much or too little protein.
  7. Describe celiac disease, including what happens in the digestive tract, the consequences, how it is diagnosed, and what foods need to be avoided.
  8. Identify the strengths and limitations of vegetarian and meat-containing diets. Identify nutrients of particular concern.

Chapter 7: The Vitamins
There will not be exam questions on biotin, pantothenic acid, choline, or "non-B vitamins."

  1. Identify the key differences between fat-soluble and water-soluble vitamins.
  2. Identify the major functions of each vitamin.
  3. Identify major food sources of each vitamin, including factors (if any) that enhance or interfere with the use of a specific vitamin.
  4. Identify the name and symptoms of each vitamin deficiency disease.
  5. Identify toxicity problems, if any, for each vitamin.
  6. Describe how to minimize nutrient losses during food storage and preparation.
  7. Identify who might benefit from multivitamin-mineral supplements and identify guidelines for selecting supplements.

Chapter 8: Water and Minerals

  1. Identify the major roles of water in the body.
  2. Describe factors influencing the amount of water needed by the body.
  3. Identify the major functions of calcium, magnesium, sodium, potassium, iodine, iron, zinc, selenium, and fluoride.
  4. Identify the major food sources of calcium, magnesium, sodium, potassium, iodine, iron, and zinc. Identify factors that affect absorption of iron.
  5. Identify the main symptoms of a deficiency of iron, iodine, and zinc.
  6. Identify the consequences of high intakes of calcium, sodium, fluoride, and iron and describe the conditions under which they occur.
  7. Identify U.S. populations at risk for mineral deficiencies and excesses.
  8. Identify risk factors for osteoporosis and recommendations for prevention and treatment.

Chapter 9: Energy Balance and Healthy Body Weight

  1. Identify the problems associated with extremes of body fatness and with central obesity, and define metabolic fitness.
  2. Describe the components of energy expenditure and the factors that affect BMR.
  3. Describe BMI and other ways to assess weight, body composition, and fat distribution, and identify their limitations. Interpret BMI & waist measurements to assess chronic disease risks.
  4. Explain what happens during moderate vs. rapid weight loss.
  5. Describe nutrients metabolized during feasting, fasting, and chronic fasting.
  6. Define successful weight loss and identify healthy strategies for weight management.
  7. Identify characteristics of a paradigm that focuses on achieving a healthy weight, rather than a particular weight loss goal.
  8. Describe the characteristics and consequences of eating disorders and the female athlete triad.

Chapter 10: Nutrients, Physical Activity, and the Body's Responses (exclude Controversy 10)

  1. Explain the benefits of and guidelines for fitness.
  2. Describe how the body derives energy from fat, carbohydrate, and protein, and adjusts the fuel mix for different conditions of intensity, duration, and training.
  3. Identify the dietary recommendations for carbohydrates, fats, protein, vitamins, and minerals for physical activity.
  4. Describe fluid needs and the recommendations/rationale for fluid intake before, during, and after exercise.
  5. Describe food intake recommendations for athletes, including pre-game meals.

Chapter 11: Diet and Health

  1. Define atherosclerosis and identify dietary, lifestyle, and blood lipid levels that are helpful or harmful for cardiovascular disease risk.
  2. Define hypertension, and identify dietary, lifestyle, and blood pressure levels that are helpful or harmful for hypertension.
  3. Describe the general process by which a cancer develops, and identify dietary and lifestyle factors that are helpful or harmful for cancer risk.
  4. Define complementary and alternative medicine and state possible benefits and risks.
  5. Describe how genes and nutrition interact and the current reality of using genetic testing to assess an individual's disease risk.

Chapter 12: Food Safety and Food Technology

  1. Describe how microbial food poisoning can be prevented and identify foods that are particularly troublesome.
  2. Identify the temperature danger zone and safe temperatures for refrigerators and freezers and for cooking foods.
  3. Describe major food processing techniques and home storage and preparation practices. Explain their effects on foods.
  4. Describe the risks, if any, of pesticides, animal drugs, and environmental contaminants.
  5. Summarize pros and cons of "organic" foods and GMOs.
  6. Describe the purposes and safety of food additives.

Chapter 13: Life Cycle Nutrition: Mother and Infant

  1. Identify changes in nutrient needs (for energy, protein, folate, vitamin B12, and iron) during pregnancy.
  2. Identify recommended weight gains for underweight, healthy weight, overweight, and obese women. Identify potential consequences of weight gain outside the recommended range.
  3. Describe fetal alcohol syndrome and the recommendations regarding alcohol intake during pregnancy.
  4. Describe food intake recommendations during pregnancy and describe ways to manage the discomforts of pregnancy.
  5. Describe the impact of maternal nutrition on breastfeeding.
  6. Identify benefits of breastfeeding, and recommendations for breastfeeding or its alternatives.
  7. Describe indicators that an infant is ready for solid foods.
  8. Describe the recommended schedule and rationale for adding specific nutrient supplements and various kinds of foods to the infant's diet between 0-12 months.
  9. Identify recommendations regarding childhood obesity.

Chapter 14: Child, Teen, and Older Adult (exclude Controversy 14)

  1. Describe appropriate feeding and eating practices related to children's developmental levels and circumstances including the principles of the Division of Responsibility.
  2. Describe the relationship between nutrition and each of the following: food allergies, hyperactivity, and dental caries
  3. Describe the nutrition needs and eating patterns of adolescence and ways to improve nutrient intake.
  4. Describe how the physical changes of aging affect nutrient needs, and describe the obstacles to good nutrition for aging people.
  5. Identify the benefits of family meals.

Chapter 15: Hunger and the Global Environment (exclude Controversy 15)

  1. Define food security.
  2. Identify possible explanations for the paradox of food poverty and obesity.
  3. Identify potential causes and consequences of food poverty in the US and the world.
  4. Describe the causes and consequences of childhood malnutrition.
  5. Identify ways to stretch food dollars and reduce waste.
  6. Describe sustainable solutions for decreasing hunger worldwide.
